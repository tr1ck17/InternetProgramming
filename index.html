Background on Philosophy: Core Branches

Epistemology

Study of knowledge; What is it? What makes it possible? Are we born with it, or is it learned?

Metaphysics

Study of reality; What is time? What is space? Is there a God? Do numbers exist? What makes a person a person?

Value Theory

Study of values and ethics; What is good, or bad? Is it okay to lie, steal, cheat, or hurt? Do we owe anything to anyone?

Logic

Study of correct reasoning; How do you prove that a logical proof is correct, or that its model is correct? How did nature produce logic? Is logical reasoning the best kind of reasoning?

Impact by AI

AI disregards many fundamental concepts within philosophy, even in its existence.

Human Potential vs Mechanical System

Noemamag.com states this: "Here humans, living organisms; open and evolving; beings that are equipped with intelligence and, thus, with interiority."
"There machines, lifeless, mechanical things; closed, determined and deterministic systems devoid of intelligence and interiority."

Things like art, existence, history, and knowledge, morals, politics, understanding, these are concepts designed to provide us with the understanding of what is means to have human potential. Therefore, everything else can be sufficiently described as a "closed, determined mechanical system"

So, the existance of AI, and the resulting philosophical significance of AI models, which are built, technical systems behaving as if intelligent. In this way, they "break the scaffold" of what's desribed above.

Moral Agents

Using AI to answer moral questions raises more questions than it does answers. For example, when you ask an AI model to align itself with "human values", it can do a multitude of things.

One of these things is to ask, "Which values?". Another is to align itself with a specific set of values that is fundamentally supported by a MAJORITY of humans, but not all.

This can lead to confusion, debate, and perhaps even a LACK of clarity when it comes to what is considered good, bad, moral or immoral.

Morality Conversation & Philosophical AI Foundations

The heart of the discussion is how to align AI behavior with human values. Interestingly enough, it seems that the philosphers of old laid the very groundwork for what designed present day AI.

Aristotle worked with formal logic, which evolved into a "structured approach to reasoning", and is a foundation of AI algorithms today.

Hundreds of years later, Descartes began debates about whether machines could ever replace humans in thought and action; he even proposed the idea that some functions of human though could be mechanized.

And Gottfried Leibniz developed the binary system, which as we all know is a core foundation of computers today, and therefore is a core foundation of Artificial Intelligence.

Altogher, it's a strange irony that the same philosophy used to create the foundations of what is AI today, is the same philosophy that AI cannot take a strong stance on.

Taking this into consideration, below are some important topics to consider when we discuss the morality of AI, and what it should be.

Deontology

Rule-based ethics: should AI follow strict moral duties regardless of outcomes?

Consequentialism

Outcome-focused ethics: can AI measure and optimize for well-being or harm?

Virtue Ethics

Character-based ethics: what would “good” behavior look like in a machine context?

Conclusion

AI forces philosophy to move from abstract questions to real-world designs and policies. By blending ethical theory with practical safeguards, we can shape intelligent systems that respect human dignity, promote fairness, and earn trust.
